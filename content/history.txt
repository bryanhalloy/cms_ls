1990L Stuff happened.

November 20, 1998: Zarya, the first module of the ISS, is launched into orbit by a Russian Proton rocket.

December 4, 1998: Unity Node, the second module and first American component, is launched and attached to Zarya, officially beginning ISS assembly.

July 12, 2000: Zvezda Service Module, providing living quarters for crew and life support systems, is launched, allowing the first long-term human habitation on the ISS.
