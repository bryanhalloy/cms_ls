Certainly, here are the key milestones and developments during the construction of the International Space Station (ISS):

1998: Launch of Zarya - The first module of the ISS, Zarya, was launched into orbit on November 20, 1998, by a Russian Proton rocket, providing power, propulsion, and guidance during the early assembly stages.

1998-2000: Unity and Zvezda Modules - Unity, the American module, was attached to Zarya in December 1998. In July 2000, the Russian Zvezda Service Module, housing living quarters and life support systems, was added, enabling long-term human habitation.
